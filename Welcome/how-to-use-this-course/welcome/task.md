# Welcome

## Why this course exists

Modern SAP development has never been easier. RAP and CAP scaffold your services
and generate your screens before your coffee is cold. AI finishes the line of
code you started typing. It feels like progress, and a lot of it genuinely is.

But I keep running into the same quiet problem. Developers can ship a Fiori app
without being able to explain a single thing the framework is doing beneath it.
The UI is generated. The bindings are generated. The structure is generated.
Then one day something breaks. A control will not render. A model will not
update. A page goes blank. And they freeze, because they have never seen the
engine under the hood they have been driving. That is not a talent problem.
Nobody ever showed them the machine.

This course is my answer to that.

I am not going to talk you out of RAP, CAP, or AI. I use all three, and so should
you. What I want is for you to understand what they are doing for you, so that you
stay in control of the tool instead of the tool quietly being in control of you.
Because the day SAPUI5 stops being magic to you, everything changes. You stop
fearing errors, because you finally know where they live. You start designing
apps that are clean and deliberate instead of whatever a generator happened to
produce. And you become the person your team comes to when the autogenerated
answer runs out.

Here is the part nobody likes to say out loud. AI can write code, but it cannot
care whether the code is right. It answers exactly what you ask of it. If you do
not understand the framework, you do not even know what to ask for, and you will
not recognise a bad answer when it hands you one. The developer who will matter
is not the one who can prompt the fastest. It is the one who knows the craft so
deeply that they can out-think the autocomplete and out-design it.

So we are going to do this the slow way, on purpose. You will build a real SAP
business application with your own two hands, one piece at a time, and you will
understand every line of it: what it is, why it exists, and what would break
without it. No magic. No black boxes. Just you and the framework, until it
belongs to you.

Now, a confession and an apology in advance. SAP does give us documentation, and
honestly it is more than a lot of frameworks bother with. Real people put real
work into it, and I am grateful for it. But I will be straight with you: there
were days when understanding one page of it sent me on a small world tour. A blog
post here. A Stack Overflow answer there. A Reddit thread, a LinkedIn comment, a
quiet "just ask at the meetup" from a colleague. By the time the concept finally
clicked, I had visited five websites and two time zones. So to whoever at SAP
wrote those docs and happens to be reading this: thank you, truly, and I am sorry
for the joke. You did the hard part. I just kept getting lost on the way to it.

And a lot of what eventually clicked, clicked because of people, not pages. To the
regulars at a SAP meetup here in Sydney that I really should get to more often: a real part of this course exists because of conversations that started
there. Community is the documentation SAP cannot ship.

So I wrote this for the person the docs sometimes quietly assume you are not. The
beginner. The curious outsider. The developer who is sharp at their craft but has
never felt quite at home in the SAP ecosystem. I will explain things in plain,
human language, the way I wish someone had explained them to me.

One last thing, and it is deliberate. We are building this in JetBrains IDEs, not
in Business Application Studio or VS Code. Not because anything is wrong with
those, because there is not, and no, I am not staging a rebellion against SAP. I
just want you to see with your own eyes that SAPUI5 is not locked inside one
toolbox. It is open. You can build it with the tools you already love. There is a
whole world outside the defaults, and you are allowed to go exploring.

And there is a hidden gift in doing it this way. Business Application Studio and
VS Code with the SAP extensions do an enormous amount for you automatically. They
scaffold the project, wire the configuration, and quietly handle a dozen steps
you never see. Because it is automatic, you never notice it, and so you never
learn to value it. Step outside those tools and you have to do those same steps
yourself, by hand. That is exactly how I finally learned the UI5 Tooling, and the
rest of the kit SAP built to make our lives easier. Doing it the manual way is
what made me understand what the automation had been doing for me all along, and
appreciate it properly. You cannot be grateful for a convenience you never knew
you had.

This is free, and it will stay free. I built it because I wished it had existed
when I was learning. If it makes even one developer genuinely dangerous, in the
best sense of that word, then it was worth every hour.

Welcome. Let us get our hands dirty.

## How to use this course

By the end of this course you will have built a complete, realistic SAP
business application from scratch: **Sales Order Management**, where a company
keeps track of its products, customers and sales orders, with the same kind of
user experience you see in professional SAP software.

You will not just read about SAPUI5 - you will write the app yourself, one small
step at a time, and understand the reason behind every line.

## Who this course is for

You should already be comfortable with **JavaScript** (and, for the TypeScript
track, **TypeScript**). This course does not teach those languages; it teaches
the SAPUI5 framework. You will also need **Node.js** (version 20 or newer) and a
JetBrains IDE with the EduTools plugin, which you already have if you can read
this.

## One-time setup (do this first)

The course uses a checker that gives you instant feedback when you press
**Check**. It needs to be installed once. Open a terminal at the project root and
run:

```
npm install
```

That single command installs both the checker and the SAPUI5 development tools
you will use to run your app in a browser. You only do this once.

## How the course is organised

This is a **guided project**. That means it is not a pile of unrelated
exercises - it is one application that grows as you go. Each lesson builds
directly on the one before, and within a lesson your code carries forward from
each step (called a *stage*) to the next. You are always working on the same,
steadily improving app.

There are two parallel tracks:

- **JavaScript** - start here. It covers the whole course.
- **TypeScript** - the same journey again in TypeScript, once you have finished
  (or if you prefer to work in TypeScript).

## How to do a task

1. **Read the description first.** Every task explains *what* you are about to
   build and, more importantly, *why* - what problem it solves and how a good
   engineer thinks about it. Read it before you touch the code. The
   understanding is the point; the typing is the easy part.
2. **Fill in the blanks.** The code file has highlighted blanks for the parts you
   need to write. Click into each one and type your answer.
3. **Press Check.** The checker runs and tells you, in plain language, what is
   correct and what still needs work.
4. **Stuck? Peek the solution.** Use **Peek author's solution** to reveal a
   correct answer, or **Reset Task** to start the file over. There is no penalty
   for looking - but try first, because you learn far more from the attempt.

## How marking works - you have real design freedom

This course is run like a country with fair laws: **strict about what is genuinely
right or wrong, free about everything else.**

- If you enter something **invalid** - a version number that does not exist, a
  setting that breaks the app, a value outside an allowed list - the check fails.
  That is a real mistake, and catching it is part of learning.
- If you make a **valid design choice** that simply differs from the author's -
  a different theme, your own title text, an extra library you wanted - the check
  **passes**. There is often more than one correct answer.

So you and another learner can hand in different-looking apps and both be right.
The goal is for you to learn to make sound decisions and design an app in your
own way, not to memorise one "official" version.

## Seeing your app run

The Check only inspects your code. To actually *see* the app in a browser, run
the SAPUI5 development server from the folder of the stage you are working on:

```
npx ui5 serve --open index.html
```

Some early stages intentionally show a blank or minimal page - the description
will tell you what to expect and why.

## Getting the most out of this

In a world where tools can generate code for you, the valuable skill is no longer
typing - it is understanding. Throughout this course, keep asking yourself:

- What is this file or setting for?
- What would break if it were missing or wrong?
- Why is this the recommended way, and what are the alternatives?

If you can answer those, you are becoming a designer of software, not just a
writer of it. That is exactly what this course is built to develop.

When you are ready, move to the **JavaScript** track and open the first lesson,
**Getting Started**. Good luck.
